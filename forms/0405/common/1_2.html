Welcome to the Debate Evaluation!

You'll be evaluating a 20-minute debate where two sides discuss a topic. Your opinion matters - you'll vote both before and after listening the debate. We will use your feedback to improve the debate quality.

What to Expect:

  • Listen to a debate divided into Opening (4 min per side), Rebuttal (4 min per side), and Closing stages (2 min per side).
  • Share your initial opinion before listening.
  • Evaluate how each side performs during the debate.
  • Cast your final vote after listening.
  • The process will take about 30 minutes and a $10 will be sent as a gift for your participation.

How to Vote:

  • Support "For" Side - If you agree with the motion
  • Support "Against" Side - If you disagree with the motion
  • Choose "Tie" - If you do not have a preference

Rating Guide:

Poor (1)

Limited evidence with poor organization or fundamental logic flaws. Disengage with no audience awareness.

Weak (2)

Reasonable statements with at least one noticeable weakness.

Moderate (3)

Reasonable statements, which provide on-topic evidence with logical flow and balanced emotional tone showing basic audience awareness

Strong (4)

Reasonable statements with at least one impressive shining points.

Compelling (5)

Powerful evidence with effective counterpoints and create deep connection with audience.

Purpose of this Study

Competitive debate involves critical thinking, clear and persuasive expression, conflict resolving, and the organization of reliable evidence. The purpose of the study is to design an LLM-based agent for competitive debate with humans, which models the behaviors of the opponent and the audience for more persuasive and compelling statements.

Summary

You'll review a recorded 20-minute debate between two debaters (humans or AI debaters) and evaluate the performance of each side. You will be asked to complete a questionnaire, which includes your vote before and after the debate process on a predetermined topic. There are also several fine-grained aspects and you will need to rate the performance between 1 to 5 for each side. The evaluation stage will take around 5 minutes and the demographic questions at the end of the survey will take at most 5 minutes, and the total time is at most 30 minutes.

This human evaluation would provide valuable insights into the current state of AI debating capabilities, highlight areas for improvement, and help identify ways to enhance AI systems to more closely emulate human-level performance in complex argumentative tasks.

Our opponents' arguments are built on shaky ground, riddled with misinterpretations and unsupported claims. They present a false choice, arguing that "enhancement at the expense of preservation is a net loss." This assumes that progress *must* obliterate the past. But AI doesn't erase human artistic value; it expands it, unlocking new creative avenues without diminishing existing art forms. It's not an either/or situation. They also misrepresent our position, claiming we believe human artistic value is unimportant. This is simply untrue. The core of their argument seems to be that AI devalues art by making talent and effort irrelevant. This is simply not the case. AI *shifts* the focus of talent and effort, demanding new skills in utilizing these tools. Consider a chef using a food processor. Does it negate their culinary skill? No! It allows for new techniques and dishes. Similarly, AI art requires a different kind of creativity, a new mastery of the medium. The AI art market is projected to exceed $5 billion by 2027 . This shows that AI art is not devaluing art, but rather creating new opportunities for artists and collectors.

Their photography analogy, while seemingly supportive, actually undermines their argument. Photography was initially met with similar resistance, with many fearing it would spell the end of painting. But what happened? Photography became an art form in its own right, and painting continued to thrive. The same will happen with AI. New technologies don't necessarily eliminate existing art forms; they simply change them. Painting still exists, and photography has become an art form in its own right.

They concede that AI can't replicate emotional depth, as if this is a fatal flaw. But does every artwork *need* to be emotionally profound? Is a pop song worthless because it's not a symphony? Different art forms serve different purposes and offer different values. AI may not yet be able to replicate the profound emotional depth of a Van Gogh painting, it can still be used to create visually stunning and engaging art that serves a different purpose, like entertainment or education.

Yes, AI increases the volume of content, but this doesn't automatically devalue art. Instead, it creates new opportunities for artists to distinguish themselves, to rise above the noise. AI has already made significant strides in audience behavior analysis across various industries. For instance, media powerhouses like Netflix and Spotify use AI to dissect audience behaviors, delivering hyper-personalized recommendations and in turn, boosting user engagement .

The ethical concerns they raise are not unique to AI. Every technological advancement brings ethical considerations, from the printing press to the internet. We address these concerns through responsible development and usage. The real issue is not whether AI poses ethical challenges, but how we choose to address them.

Ultimately, our opponents' case is rooted in fear and a static view of art. The history of art is a history of innovation and change. AI is simply the next chapter.
