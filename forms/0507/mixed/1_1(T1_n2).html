Welcome to the Debate Evaluation!

You'll be evaluating a debate where two sides discuss a topic. Your opinion matters - you'll vote how persuasive each side is in each stage. We will use your feedback to improve the debate quality.

What to Expect:

Debate Structure

The full debate includes:

  • Opening: 4 min audio per side
  • Rebuttal: 4 min audio per side
  • Closing: 2 min audio per side

You'll evaluate a portion of this debate.

Your Evaluation Tasks

For each stage, you'll:

  • Rate the persuasiveness of each side's statements
  • Update your position after hearing each argument
  • Provide optional feedback
Final Comparison

In the final stage:

  • You'll see two versions of each side's closing statement
  • Rate each version independently
  • Select which version you found more persuasive
Important: Before beginning, you'll vote for the side you initially support. After each stage, you'll have the opportunity to reconsider and update your position based on the arguments presented.
Note: Throughout the evaluation, you'll encounter attention check questions to ensure data quality. Participants who demonstrate thoughtful engagement will receive compensation as agreed. If you're unable to commit to providing quality responses, you may exit the survey at any time without penalty.

Rating Guide for Persuasiveness:

1
Poor

Limited evidence with poor organization or fundamental logic flaws. Disengage with no audience awareness.

2
Weak

Reasonable statements with at least one noticeable weakness.

3
Moderate

Reasonable statements, which provide on-topic evidence with logical flow and balanced emotional tone showing basic audience awareness

4
Strong

Reasonable statements with at least one impressive shining points.

5
Compelling

Powerful evidence with effective counterpoints and create deep connection with audience.

Purpose of this Study

Competitive debate involves critical thinking, clear and persuasive expression, conflict resolving, and the organization of reliable evidence. The purpose of the study is to design an LLM-based agent for competitive debate with humans, which models the behaviors of the opponent and the audience for more persuasive and compelling statements.

Summary

You'll review a recorded 20-minute debate between two debaters (humans or AI debaters) and evaluate the performance of each side. You will be asked to complete a questionnaire, which includes your vote before and after the debate process on a predetermined topic. There are also several fine-grained aspects and you will need to rate the performance between 1 to 5 for each side. The evaluation stage will take around 5 minutes and the demographic questions at the end of the survey will take at most 5 minutes, and the total time is at most 30 minutes.

This human evaluation would provide valuable insights into the current state of AI debating capabilities, highlight areas for improvement, and help identify ways to enhance AI systems to more closely emulate human-level performance in complex argumentative tasks.

Good morning, everyone. We're here today to discuss a critical issue impacting the financial stability of the United States: the debt ceiling. To ensure clarity, when we say "Congress should abolish the debt ceiling," we mean the complete elimination of the statutory limit on the total amount of money the federal government can borrow to meet its *existing* legal obligations. This allows the Treasury to pay for what Congress has *already* approved, regardless of current economic conditions or political climates.

Our position is that the most important judging criterion today should be which side provides the most beneficial outcome for the U.S. economy. We firmly believe that abolishing the debt ceiling is the clear path to that beneficial outcome.

First, abolishing the debt ceiling significantly reduces the risk of a U.S. default and avoids potentially catastrophic economic consequences. The debt ceiling creates a situation where the U.S. could fail to pay its bills, not because it lacks the resources, but because of political gridlock. Treasury Secretary Janet Yellen warned in *a letter to Congress in 2023* that a default could "cause irreparable harm to the U.S. economy, the livelihoods of all Americans, and global financial stability" . *Foreign Policy reported in 2023* that economists predict a default could cause U.S. stock prices to fall by one-fifth and significantly contract the economy . It's a risk we simply cannot afford to take. Removing the debt ceiling is a responsible step toward preventing such a disaster. Furthermore, the political brinkmanship surrounding the debt ceiling has real costs. Instead of citing *Wikipedia*, let's consider that the debt ceiling crisis of 2011 raised borrowing costs for the government.

Second, abolishing the debt ceiling aligns congressional actions with previously approved spending, streamlining governmental processes. The current system forces Congress to vote twice on spending decisions. First, they authorize the spending. Then, they have to vote again to raise the debt ceiling to pay for it. This is redundant and creates unnecessary political drama. As House Budget Committee Chairman John Yarmuth pointed out in *an interview with the House Budget Committee in February 2021*, the debt ceiling "certainly hasn’t constrained the debt in any way. All it’s done is open up opportunities for mischief and brinksmanship" . Removing the debt ceiling streamlines the process, ensuring that the government can fulfill its financial obligations without these artificial constraints. Some argue that the debt ceiling acts as a check on government spending. However, Congress retains full control over the budget through the appropriations process. The debt ceiling simply forces them to authorize payment for spending they have already approved.

Third, removing the debt ceiling reinforces the full faith and credit of the United States. The debt ceiling undermines our reputation as a reliable borrower. As *the World Economic Forum reported in 2024*, failure to pay bondholders would prompt credit rating agencies to downgrade Treasury debt, leading to higher borrowing costs for the government, businesses, and households . Abolishing the debt ceiling sends a clear signal that the U.S. is committed to meeting its obligations, strengthening our financial standing on the world stage.

In conclusion, abolishing the debt ceiling is a necessary step to protect our economy, streamline government operations, and maintain our credibility. Contact your representatives and urge them to support the abolishment of the debt ceiling, securing America's financial future. Thank you.

(Optional) Against - Transcript
In this debate, we shall argue that Congress should not abolish the debt ceiling. The debt ceiling is a legislative limit on the amount of national debt that the U.S. Treasury can incur, limiting the amount of money the federal government can borrow to pay its existing legal obligations, including Social Security and Medicare benefits, military salaries, national debt interest, tax refunds, and other payments. This definition is important because it clarifies that the debt ceiling doesn't authorize new spending, but rather allows the government to pay for existing obligations already approved by Congress.

Our judging criteria is fiscal stability and responsible governance. The most desirable outcome is one that promotes long-term fiscal stability and responsible governance, ensuring the U.S. government meets its obligations without jeopardizing the economic well-being of its citizens or the global economy. We should evaluate whether abolishing the debt ceiling contributes to or detracts from this stability and responsibility.

First, the debt ceiling promotes fiscal responsibility by requiring legislative consent, giving Congress oversight authority . The Council on Foreign Relations notes that the debt limit requires legislative consent, providing Congress some oversight authority and engendering fiscal accountability . Without the debt ceiling, there would be less incentive for Congress to control spending, potentially leading to a rapid accumulation of debt. Economist De Rugy argues that the debt ceiling helps keep the budget normalized, stating that "Congress has behaved irresponsibly for years" . Abolishing the debt ceiling would remove a critical check on government spending, fostering an environment of fiscal irresponsibility and potentially increasing the national debt.

Second, the debt ceiling encourages negotiation and compromise on fiscal policy, leading to more considered decisions. While often contentious, the debt ceiling forces dialogue and potential agreements on spending and revenue. Removing this pressure could result in unilateral actions and less fiscal restraint. Scott Peters and Bill Huizenga reintroduced the Responsible Budgeting Act , which would create new mechanisms to raise the debt ceiling while promoting fiscally responsible solutions . This act demonstrates that the debt ceiling can be a catalyst for bipartisan efforts to address fiscal challenges. Thus, maintaining the debt ceiling is crucial for fostering a balanced and responsible approach to fiscal policy.

Third, abolishing the debt ceiling could undermine confidence in U.S. debt and trigger economic instability. Removing this fiscal restraint could signal to investors that the U.S. is not committed to fiscal discipline, potentially leading to higher borrowing costs and a financial crisis. As the Brookings Institution points out, eliminating the debt ceiling could lead to higher borrowing costs and an increased risk of economic recession . The Committee for a Responsible Federal Budget warns that removing the debt ceiling could lead to default, higher interest rates, and a severe economic downturn, risking global financial instability and significant job losses . Thus, the debt ceiling provides a necessary safeguard against economic instability and maintains investor confidence.

In conclusion, maintaining the debt ceiling is essential for fiscal responsibility, encouraging negotiation, and preserving economic stability. Abolishing it would remove a crucial check on government spending, undermine investor confidence, and potentially trigger a financial crisis. For these reasons, we stand firmly against abolishing the debt ceiling.
