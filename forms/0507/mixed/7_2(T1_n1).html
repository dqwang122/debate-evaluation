Welcome to the Debate Evaluation!

You'll be evaluating a debate where two sides discuss a topic. Your opinion matters - you'll vote how persuasive each side is in each stage. We will use your feedback to improve the debate quality.

What to Expect:

Debate Structure

The full debate includes:

  • Opening: 4 min audio per side
  • Rebuttal: 4 min audio per side
  • Closing: 2 min audio per side

You'll evaluate a portion of this debate.

Your Evaluation Tasks

For each stage, you'll:

  • Rate the persuasiveness of each side's statements
  • Update your position after hearing each argument
  • Provide optional feedback
Final Comparison

In the final stage:

  • You'll see two versions of each side's closing statement
  • Rate each version independently
  • Select which version you found more persuasive
Important: Before beginning, you'll vote for the side you initially support. After each stage, you'll have the opportunity to reconsider and update your position based on the arguments presented.
Note: Throughout the evaluation, you'll encounter attention check questions to ensure data quality. Participants who demonstrate thoughtful engagement will receive compensation as agreed. If you're unable to commit to providing quality responses, you may exit the survey at any time without penalty.

Rating Guide for Persuasiveness:

1
Poor

Limited evidence with poor organization or fundamental logic flaws. Disengage with no audience awareness.

2
Weak

Reasonable statements with at least one noticeable weakness.

3
Moderate

Reasonable statements, which provide on-topic evidence with logical flow and balanced emotional tone showing basic audience awareness

4
Strong

Reasonable statements with at least one impressive shining points.

5
Compelling

Powerful evidence with effective counterpoints and create deep connection with audience.

In this debate, we argue that dating expenses should not be shared equally between partners. We define **dating expenses** as costs incurred during courtship activities, including but not limited to meals, entertainment, transportation, and gifts. **Shared equally** means dividing the total dating expenses precisely in half, with each partner contributing 50%.

Our judging criteria is maximizing relationship satisfaction and longevity. The approach to dating expenses that best promotes long-term relationship satisfaction, stability, and minimizes potential conflict should be favored.

First, equal splitting ignores income disparity and creates unfair burdens. Forcing equal splits disproportionately impacts lower-income individuals, creating financial strain and resentment that undermines relationship quality. A LendingTree study showed that many people find dating expenses challenging, with 62% of respondents citing their finances as a significant obstacle to having a fulfilling dating life . Requiring someone with a lower income to contribute 50% of dating expenses can be a significant burden, leading to stress and resentment. As noted in The Financial Diet, splitting expenses evenly in a relationship where one person makes significantly more than the other is unfair, especially when building a financial future together . This demonstrates that strict equality doesn't account for differing financial circumstances, negatively impacting relationship quality.

Second, equal splitting undermines traditional courting norms and can be perceived as unromantic. Insisting on a strict 50/50 split can diminish the perceived generosity and chivalry traditionally associated with courtship, potentially impacting attraction and relationship dynamics. Slate reports that traditionally, men paid most dating expenses, and while modern views favor splitting costs equally, many still adhere to traditional norms . This highlights that completely dismissing traditional courting gestures can be detrimental to some relationships, as it can be interpreted as a lack of interest or investment, especially when 76% of men reported feeling guilty in accepting women’s money.

Third, flexible and equitable alternatives better promote relationship harmony. Alternatives such as income-based sharing, alternating payments, or covering different expenses allow for more customized and equitable arrangements that better reflect individual circumstances and preferences, fostering greater relationship satisfaction. CNBC reports that many couples prefer flexible arrangements over strict splitting, with experts suggesting clear communication and taking turns . For example, Female Invest suggests calculating the percentage of each partner's total income and dividing shared expenses accordingly, allowing for a more nuanced and fair approach . This approach acknowledges different financial capabilities and prevents one partner from feeling unfairly burdened, ultimately fostering greater relationship satisfaction.

In conclusion, sharing dating expenses equally is not the optimal approach. It ignores income disparities, undermines traditional norms, and is less effective than flexible alternatives in promoting relationship satisfaction and longevity.
