Debate Output Comparison

You'll be comparing two different debate outputs side by side. Please evaluate both versions and provide ratings for each.

What to Expect:

  • Listen to a debate divided into Opening (4 min per side), Rebuttal (4 min per side), and Closing stages (2 min per side).
  • Share your initial opinion before listening.
  • Evaluate how each side performs during the debate.
  • Cast your final vote after listening.
  • The process will take about 1 hours and a $30 Amazon Gift Card will be sent as a gift for your participation.

How to Vote:

  • Support "For" Side - If you agree with the motion
  • Support "Against" Side - If you disagree with the motion
  • Choose "Tie" - If you do not have a preference

Rating Guide:

Poor (1)

Limited evidence with poor organization or fundamental logic flaws. Disengage with no audience awareness.

Weak (2)

Reasonable statements with at least one noticeable weakness.

Moderate (3)

Reasonable statements, which provide on-topic evidence with logical flow and balanced emotional tone.

Strong (4)

Reasonable statements with at least one impressive shining point.

Compelling (5)

Powerful evidence with effective counterpoints and deep audience connection.

(Optional) For - Transcript A
In this debate, we shall argue that dating expenses should be shared equally between partners regardless of gender. We define **dating expenses** as the costs incurred during a date, including but not limited to meals, entertainment, transportation, and gifts. **Shared equally** means dividing dating expenses in half, with each partner contributing 50% of the total cost, regardless of income or gender.

Our judging criteria is that the most equitable dating practice is the one that best promotes gender equality and minimizes financial strain on both partners.

First, equal sharing of dating expenses promotes gender equality by dismantling traditional gender roles. Historically, societal norms have placed the financial burden of dating primarily on men. This reinforces outdated stereotypes and contributes to what some social scientists call "benevolent sexism" – the idea that women need to be looked after by men . A recent article in the *Daily Nexus* argues directly against gender roles without a scientific basis, stating that expecting one gender to pay more than the other is arbitrary . By sharing expenses equally, we actively challenge these antiquated norms and foster a more equitable dynamic, promoting autonomy for both partners . Sharing expenses can lead to a more egalitarian footing from the very beginning .

Second, equal sharing of dating expenses reduces potential power imbalances in relationships. When one partner consistently bears the financial burden, it can inadvertently create a sense of obligation or dependence, leading to unequal power dynamics. As noted in *Digital Commons*, equal dating expenses promote fairness and reduce power imbalances . Sharing expenses equally fosters mutual respect and autonomy. A 26-year-old woman interviewed in *Digital Commons* stated, “I think if you expect equality in your relationship, then there should be equality when paying for dates ." Sharing financial responsibility fosters a sense of partnership and shared decision-making, leading to a more balanced and healthy relationship.

Third, equal sharing of dating expenses promotes financial responsibility and open communication. Openly discussing and sharing expenses encourages couples to be more transparent about their financial situations and priorities. This transparency builds trust and strengthens the foundation of the relationship. Furthermore, sharing expenses can reduce financial stress and make money seem less tricky and more manageable . When both partners are invested financially, it encourages them to communicate effectively about their needs and expectations, leading to better financial planning and a stronger foundation for a long-term relationship. A survey by Verivox and Parship found that 35% of adults cite money as a major source of conflict in romantic relationships . By addressing financial matters upfront through equal sharing, couples can proactively mitigate potential conflicts and build a more resilient partnership.

In conclusion, we firmly believe that dating expenses should be shared equally between partners regardless of gender. This approach promotes gender equality, reduces power imbalances, and fosters financial responsibility and open communication, all of which are essential for building strong, healthy, and equitable relationships.
