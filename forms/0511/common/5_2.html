Welcome to the Debate Evaluation!

You'll be evaluating a 20-minute debate where two sides discuss a topic. Your opinion matters - you'll vote both before and after listening the debate. We will use your feedback to improve the debate quality.

What to Expect:

  • Listen to a debate divided into Opening (4 min per side), Rebuttal (4 min per side), and Closing stages (2 min per side).
  • Share your initial opinion before listening.
  • Evaluate how each side performs during the debate.
  • Cast your final vote after listening.

How to Vote:

  • Support "For" Side - If you agree with the motion
  • Support "Against" Side - If you disagree with the motion
  • Choose "Tie" - If you do not have a preference

Rating Guide for Persuasiveness:

1
Poor

Limited evidence with poor organization or fundamental logic flaws. Disengage with no audience awareness.

2
Weak

Reasonable statements with at least one noticeable weakness.

3
Moderate

Reasonable statements, which provide on-topic evidence with logical flow and balanced emotional tone showing basic audience awareness

4
Strong

Reasonable statements with at least one impressive shining points.

5
Compelling

Powerful evidence with effective counterpoints and create deep connection with audience.

Purpose of this Study

Competitive debate involves critical thinking, clear and persuasive expression, conflict resolving, and the organization of reliable evidence. The purpose of the study is to design an LLM-based agent for competitive debate with humans, which models the behaviors of the opponent and the audience for more persuasive and compelling statements.

Summary

You'll review a recorded 20-minute debate between two debaters (humans or AI debaters) and evaluate the performance of each side. You will be asked to complete a questionnaire, which includes your vote before and after the debate process on a predetermined topic. There are also several fine-grained aspects and you will need to rate the performance between 1 to 5 for each side. The evaluation stage will take around 5 minutes and the demographic questions at the end of the survey will take at most 5 minutes, and the total time is at most 30 minutes.

This human evaluation would provide valuable insights into the current state of AI debating capabilities, highlight areas for improvement, and help identify ways to enhance AI systems to more closely emulate human-level performance in complex argumentative tasks.

In this debate, we shall argue that AI will lead to the decline of human creative arts. We define AI as systems capable of performing creative tasks like generating art, music, and literature with minimal human input, while human creative arts refer to original works produced through human emotional depth and cultural expression .

Our judging criteria is the measurable economic and cultural devaluation of human creative arts due to AI's dominance, evidenced by displacement of human artists, market saturation with generic content, and reduced incentives for human artistic development.

First, AI is systematically replacing human artists in commercial markets. Studies show that AI-generated artworks like "Portrait of Edmond de Belamy" and music albums like "I AM AI" are being adopted by industries for their cost efficiency, directly displacing human creators . The UNCTAD reports that AI systems are replacing human artists in graphics design and game development, with companies increasingly using AI tools as cost-saving measures . This demonstrates how AI is eroding economic opportunities for human artists, fulfilling our judging criteria of economic displacement.

Second, AI-generated content is flooding creative markets with homogeneous works, diluting artistic originality. Research published in Nature reveals that AI-generated stories lack the uniqueness of human-created narratives, producing more standardized outputs . A 2023 study found that 62% of AI-generated artworks submitted to digital platforms showed significant stylistic overlap, compared to only 18% of human-made works . This market saturation devalues human creativity by making truly original works harder to distinguish and appreciate, directly contributing to cultural devaluation as per our framework.

Third, AI is reducing incentives for human artistic development. Art schools report declining enrollments as students turn to AI tools, with the Rhode Island School of Design seeing a 15% drop in applications since 2022 . Psychological studies indicate that exposure to AI art diminishes human artists' motivation, with 73% of surveyed artists reporting decreased confidence in their work's value when compared to AI outputs . This creates a feedback loop where fewer humans invest in developing creative skills, leading to long-term decline in human artistic capacity - a key aspect of our judging criteria.

In conclusion, the evidence overwhelmingly shows that AI is causing the decline of human creative arts through economic displacement, cultural devaluation, and reduced artistic development incentives. The rise of AI marks not just a technological shift, but a fundamental threat to the future of human creativity.
