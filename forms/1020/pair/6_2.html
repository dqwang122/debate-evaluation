Welcome to the Debate Evaluation!

You'll be evaluating a 20-minute debate where two sides discuss a topic. Your opinion matters - you'll vote both before and after listening the debate. We will use your feedback to improve the debate quality.

What to Expect:

  • Listen to a debate divided into Opening (4 min per side), Rebuttal (4 min per side), and Closing stages (2 min per side).
  • Share your initial opinion before listening.
  • Evaluate how each side performs during the debate.
  • Cast your final vote after listening.
  • The process will take about 1 hour and a $30 Amazon gift card will be sent via email as a gift for your participation.

How to Vote:

  • Support "For" Side - If you agree with the motion
  • Support "Against" Side - If you disagree with the motion
  • Choose "Tie" - If you do not have a preference

Rating Guide:

Limited (1)

Basic claims are provided, but there is no evidence or reasoning to influence your view. Or, the evidence provided is off-topic or contradicts the claims.

Weak (2)

Some evidence or reasoning is present, but the arguments are not sufficient or poorly organized, limiting their persuasive impact.

Moderate (3)

On-topic evidence and logical reasoning are used, moderately swaying your opinion toward the given position.

Strong (4)

Strong evidence and clear logic substantially shift your perspective, though minor gaps may remain.

Compelling (5)

Compelling evidence, effective counterpoints, and powerful reasoning strongly convince you to support the given position.

My esteemed colleagues on the opposing side raise some interesting points, but I believe they cling to an outdated vision of success and progress. While they tout the economic opportunities of big cities, they fail to acknowledge the harsh realities faced by many young people trying to survive in those very environments. Yes, big cities boast a concentration of industries, but at what cost? Competition for entry-level positions is fierce, often demanding years of experience and advanced degrees just for a foot in the door. Even when successful, the exorbitant cost of living in most major cities eats away at any salary gains, leaving young professionals struggling to afford basic necessities, let alone achieve financial stability. This is not opportunity, it's a treadmill.

Conversely, smaller cities and towns across the country are experiencing a renaissance. They are attracting specialized industries, offering unique career paths without the cutthroat competition. Couple this with a significantly lower cost of living, and suddenly, a fulfilling career with financial security becomes an attainable goal for young people.

My opponents further argue that big city life equates to intellectual stimulation and personal growth. But let us not mistake mere exposure to diversity with genuine intellectual engagement. The constant sensory overload and relentless pace of city life can be overwhelming, leading to burnout rather than intellectual curiosity. True personal growth flourishes in environments that allow for reflection, meaningful connections, and active community participation, something often absent in the anonymous hustle of big city life.

And here lies the crucial point: innovation and progress are not confined to the borders of our urban centers. In fact, many of the world’s most pressing problems are finding solutions not in towering skyscrapers, but in the hearts of smaller communities. Rural areas are at the forefront of sustainable living practices, local food movements, and community-based initiatives, fostering a new generation of environmentally conscious, problem-solving leaders.

The desire to connect with nature, reduce environmental impact, and embrace a more sustainable lifestyle is not a romantic notion for today’s youth; it is a driving force. Rural areas offer the opportunity to live in closer harmony with nature, minimize their ecological footprint, and engage in sustainable practices that directly combat the climate crisis. This direct engagement fosters a deeper sense of environmental responsibility and empowers young people to become active stewards of our planet, a critical need often overlooked in the consumer-driven culture of many urban environments.

In conclusion, while our opponents paint a picture of big city allure, we must look beyond the superficial gleam. The true path to a brighter future for our generation lies not in perpetuating the cycle of unsustainable urban living, but in embracing the potential of smaller communities. There, amidst the open spaces and close-knit connections, young people can find affordable living, fulfilling careers, and a deeper sense of purpose in building a more sustainable and equitable world. We must be brave enough to forge a new path, one that leads us not back to the concrete jungle, but towards a future filled with opportunity, connection, and genuine progress.
