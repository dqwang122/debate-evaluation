Welcome to the Debate Evaluation!

You'll be evaluating a 20-minute debate where two sides discuss a topic. Your opinion matters - you'll vote both before and after listening the debate. We will use your feedback to improve the debate quality.

What to Expect:

  • Listen to a debate divided into Opening (4 min per side), Rebuttal (4 min per side), and Closing stages (2 min per side).
  • Share your initial opinion before listening.
  • Evaluate how each side performs during the debate.
  • Cast your final vote after listening.
  • The process will take about 1 hour and a $30 Amazon gift card will be sent via email as a gift for your participation.

How to Vote:

  • Support "For" Side - If you agree with the motion
  • Support "Against" Side - If you disagree with the motion
  • Choose "Tie" - If you do not have a preference

Rating Guide:

Limited (1)

Basic claims are provided, but there is no evidence or reasoning to influence your view. Or, the evidence provided is off-topic or contradicts the claims.

Weak (2)

Some evidence or reasoning is present, but the arguments are not sufficient or poorly organized, limiting their persuasive impact.

Moderate (3)

On-topic evidence and logical reasoning are used, moderately swaying your opinion toward the given position.

Strong (4)

Strong evidence and clear logic substantially shift your perspective, though minor gaps may remain.

Compelling (5)

Compelling evidence, effective counterpoints, and powerful reasoning strongly convince you to support the given position.

Good morning, esteemed judges, fellow debaters, and audience members. Today, I stand before you to articulate why social media should not be required to verify user identities. The debate on social media identity verification is multifaceted, touching on privacy, freedom of expression, and accessibility. I will present three fundamental claims to support our stance: the protection of privacy and anonymity, the safeguarding of freedom of expression, and the promotion of accessibility and diversity.

Firstly, anonymity is a cornerstone of online privacy and plays a fundamental role in protecting individuals. In an increasingly digital world, personal data has become incredibly vulnerable to breaches, and mandating identity verification only exacerbates this risk. Requiring users to verify their identities compels them to surrender personal information, which then becomes susceptible to hacking and misuse. This not only compromises individuals’ safety but also makes social media platforms prime targets for cybercriminals. Furthermore, anonymity is vital for users who face genuine threats in their physical environment. It offers a shield for activists, whistleblowers, and individuals living under oppressive regimes, who risk persecution if their true identities were disclosed. By maintaining the option for anonymity, social media platforms serve as vital spaces for these individuals to share information and voice concerns without fear of reprisal.

Second, freedom of expression is at the heart of social media’s value, and imposing identity verification threatens this fundamental right. Social media is a tool that allows individuals to share ideas, debate issues, and engage in discourse across wide audiences. By mandating identity verification, we impose limitations on this exchange of ideas, deterring individuals from engaging openly due to fear of being tracked, exposed, or penalized. This is particularly concerning in societies where freedom of speech is not fully guaranteed. The necessity of verifying identities would stifle dissenting voices, curtail political activism, and discourage individuals from discussing sensitive or controversial topics. Protecting the freedom of expression enables diverse perspectives to be voiced and challenges oppressive norms, fostering a more informed and progressive society.

Finally, social media thrives on diversity and inclusivity, and mandatory identity verification poses a barrier to accessibility. Imposing stringent identification processes can exclude marginalized communities who may not have access to the necessary documentation or feel secure providing it. This disproportionately affects economically disadvantaged individuals, refugees, and those living in areas with limited infrastructure. Additionally, social media provides an important platform for underrepresented groups, including LGBTQ+ youth, to find community and support. For many, anonymity offers a safe means to explore identity and connect with others without fear of judgment or discrimination. By not mandating identity verification, social media platforms remain inclusive and accessible, ensuring broad participation and representation from all walks of life.

In conclusion, while the issue of online identity on social media is complex, the unintended consequences of mandating identity verification cannot be overlooked. Prioritizing the preservation of privacy and anonymity, safeguarding freedom of expression, and promoting accessibility and inclusivity are imperative. Enforcing identity verification threatens these principles, undermining the very essence of what makes social media a transformative tool for connection and communication. We must, therefore, reject the notion of mandating identity verification on social media, to maintain a digital environment where safety, freedom, and accessibility are championed. Thank you.
