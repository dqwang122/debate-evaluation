Welcome to the Debate Evaluation!

You'll be evaluating a 20-minute debate where two sides discuss a topic. Your opinion matters - you'll vote both before and after listening the debate. We will use your feedback to improve the debate quality.

What to Expect:

  • Listen to a debate divided into Opening (4 min per side), Rebuttal (4 min per side), and Closing stages (2 min per side).
  • Share your initial opinion before listening.
  • Evaluate how each side performs during the debate.
  • Cast your final vote after listening.
  • The process will take about 1 hours and a $30 Amazon Gift Card will be sent as a gift for your participation.

How to Vote:

  • Support "For" Side - If you agree with the motion
  • Support "Against" Side - If you disagree with the motion
  • Choose "Tie" - If you do not have a preference

Rating Guide:

Poor (1)

Limited evidence with poor organization or fundamental logic flaws. Disengage with no audience awareness.

Weak (2)

Reasonable statements with at least one noticeable weakness.

Moderate (3)

Reasonable statements, which provide on-topic evidence with logical flow and balanced emotional tone showing basic audience awareness

Strong (4)

Reasonable statements with at least one impressive shining points.

Compelling (5)

Powerful evidence with effective counterpoints and create deep connection with audience.

Purpose of this Study

Competitive debate involves critical thinking, clear and persuasive expression, conflict resolving, and the organization of reliable evidence. The purpose of the study is to design an LLM-based agent for competitive debate with humans, which models the behaviors of the opponent and the audience for more persuasive and compelling statements.

Summary

You'll review a recorded 20-minute debate between two debaters (humans or AI debaters) and evaluate the performance of each side. You will be asked to complete a questionnaire, which includes your vote before and after the debate process on a predetermined topic. There are also several fine-grained aspects and you will need to rate the performance between 1 to 5 for each side. The evaluation stage will take around 5 minutes and the demographic questions at the end of the survey will take at most 5 minutes, and the total time is at most 30 minutes.

This human evaluation would provide valuable insights into the current state of AI debating capabilities, highlight areas for improvement, and help identify ways to enhance AI systems to more closely emulate human-level performance in complex argumentative tasks.

In this debate, we shall argue that banning certain inappropriate books in schools is necessary to protect child welfare and promote a safe learning environment. We define "inappropriate books" as those containing explicit depictions or endorsements of sex, violence, or drug use that are deemed unsuitable for children and adolescents due to their potential to cause harm or negatively influence their development. "School" refers to educational institutions catering to children and adolescents, from elementary to high school levels. Our judging criteria is protecting child welfare. The side that best demonstrates how their position promotes the well-being and healthy development of children in schools should win the debate.

First, exposure to explicit sexual content can lead to negative attitudes and behaviors. Studies show a correlation between exposure to sexually explicit material and increased sexual aggression and risky sexual behavior in minors . Specifically, research indicates that early exposure to sexually graphic material is likely to have a negative influence and a potentially harmful impact on children . Jenni White, a former public school science teacher, stated that “Numerous studies on the use of graphic material by students indicate negative psychological effects,” including having “more casual sex partners and having sex at younger ages” . By banning books containing such content, we directly protect students from these potential harms, promoting their well-being and contributing to a healthier developmental trajectory. Therefore, limiting access to sexually explicit material is a proactive step toward safeguarding our children's future.

Second, exposure to violence in literature can contribute to desensitization and increased aggression in adolescents. Adolescents are exposed to about 30.23 acts of aggression for every hour of reading popular novels . Studies by the American Academy of Pediatrics have found that exposure to violence in media, including books, can impact kids by making them act aggressively and desensitizing them to violence . Further research indicates that adolescents with higher violence acceptance were more likely to be rejected by their classmates than adolescents with low violence acceptance . Banning books with graphic depictions of violence can help create a safer and more supportive learning environment, as violence desensitization poses a risk to children’s mental health. By reducing this exposure, we can foster a more positive and nurturing atmosphere, conducive to better learning outcomes and social interactions.

Third, parents have the right to guide their children's education and protect them from inappropriate content. While schools aim to provide diverse materials, parental rights are paramount in safeguarding their children's well-being. If books with inappropriate material are available in libraries, children or teens can be exposed to books their parents wouldn’t approve of before the parents even find out what their children are reading . In fact, on the question of book bans in schools and school libraries, 63% of parents agree that bans infringe on their rights as parents . Parental rights are already in place in most school districts: if parents do not want their child to read a text that the class has been assigned, they can ask for an alternative option, and if they do not want their child to check out specific books from the school library, they can ask for restrictions to be placed on their own children’s access . Banning inappropriate books empowers parents to ensure their children are not exposed to material that conflicts with their values or beliefs.

Against Side
(Optional) Against - Transcript
Imagine a classroom where the doors to new worlds are slammed shut, where diverse voices are silenced, and where students are discouraged from thinking for themselves. This is the chilling reality we risk creating by banning books in schools. We stand firmly against banning specific books, particularly those addressing complex topics like sex, violence, and drug use. Let's start by dissecting what our opponents define as "inappropriate books"—those they claim are "unsuitable for children and adolescents due to their potential to cause harm or negatively influence their development." This definition is not only vague but also deeply subjective. What one person deems harmful, another might recognize as a valuable opportunity for learning and personal growth.

First, banning books severely limits students' freedom to explore diverse ideas and perspectives. Sheltering children from challenging content actually hinders their ability to develop crucial critical thinking skills. As highlighted in a *2024 study by American University*, controversial literature is vital in developing critical thinking skills, encouraging students to analyze complex themes, challenge societal norms, and engage in meaningful discussions. The study *found that exposure to challenging content significantly enhances students' ability to analyze complex issues*. Banning books diminishes the quality of education students have access to and restricts their exposure to important perspectives. These perspectives include different cultures, lifestyles, and experiences, which are important for students to understand and respect. Exposure to diverse viewpoints, even those considered controversial, allows students to analyze, evaluate, and form their own opinions. Banning books stifles this intellectual growth, directly contradicting the goal of preparing students for informed participation in society. According to *research in 2023*, 78 percent of students are reading more when given the choice to read banned books, *showing that they are more engaged to read those types of content*.

Second, we firmly believe that teachers are trained professionals fully capable of selecting appropriate materials for their students. Trusting educators' expertise in curriculum development and age-appropriate content allows for flexible and responsive learning experiences tailored to specific student needs. The *National Coalition against Censorship* rightly points out that decisions about classroom materials should be based on professional judgment and standards, not individual preferences.

Third, banning books simply does not address the root causes of societal problems like violence and drug use. Focusing solely on censorship ignores the complex social, economic, and environmental factors that contribute to these issues, ultimately hindering the development of effective solutions. *Sonya Douglass, Professor of Education Leadership at Teachers College*, explains in *2023* that book bans diminish the quality of education students have access to and restrict their exposure to important perspectives. The opposition's strategy of banning books assumes that removing content eliminates the problem. However, censorship often backfires. Banned books often become more desirable and sought after. Removing books doesn't address the root causes of issues like violence or drug use.

In conclusion, banning books is not a solution; it's a disservice to our students. Instead, let's empower them with the tools they need to navigate the complexities of the world, fostering empathy, critical thinking, and a lifelong love of learning.
