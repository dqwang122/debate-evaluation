Welcome to the Debate Evaluation!

You'll be evaluating a 20-minute debate where two sides discuss a topic. Your opinion matters - you'll vote both before and after listening the debate. We will use your feedback to improve the debate quality.

What to Expect:

  • Listen to a debate divided into Opening (4 min per side), Rebuttal (4 min per side), and Closing stages (2 min per side).
  • Share your initial opinion before listening.
  • Evaluate how each side performs during the debate.
  • Cast your final vote after listening.
  • The process will take about 1 hour and a $30 Amazon gift card will be sent via email as a gift for your participation.

How to Vote:

  • Support "For" Side - If you agree with the motion
  • Support "Against" Side - If you disagree with the motion
  • Choose "Tie" - If you do not have a preference

Rating Guide:

Poor (1)

Claims lack evidence/reasoning or contradictory evidence. Tone is flat/disengaging with no audience awareness.

Weak (2)

Limited evidence with poor organization and inconsistent emotional delivery.

Moderate (3)

On-topic evidence with logical flow and balanced emotional tone showing basic audience awareness, moderately swaying your opinion toward the given position.

Strong (4)

Substantial evidence and clear logic. Engaging language that resonates with audience value. Substantially shift your perspective, though minor gaps may remain.

Compelling (5)

Powerful evidence with effective counterpoints and masterful emotional appeal that creates deep connection. Strongly convince you to support the given position.

Escaping big cities dramatically improves affordability, especially when it comes to homeownership. Data shows that rural homeownership rates are over 20% higher than in urban areas. And with the rise of remote work, young people can maintain competitive salaries while enjoying a lower cost of living, accelerating their path to financial security and owning their own home.

Think about your kids playing outside, exploring the woods, or simply breathing fresh air. Research by Bowers and colleagues, published in a study titled "Nature as an Ecological Asset for Positive Youth Development," highlights the positive impact of "nearby nature" on youth development, especially in rural settings where access is often "just beyond the doorstep." This easy access means more opportunities for outdoor recreation, leading to greater resilience and overall well-being. While cities may have some parks, access is often limited by cost, availability, and sheer distance. The Center for American Progress calls this the “nature gap.” Escaping big cities bridges that gap, giving young people a real advantage in experiencing the proven benefits of nature and outdoor recreation.

Finally, let's address *safety*. We all want to feel safe and secure in our communities. Studies consistently show that rural areas experience significantly less violent crime than urban areas. In 2021, violent crime rates in urban settings were 24.5 incidents per 1,000 people. In rural areas, it was 11.1 per 1,000. That's more than double the risk in cities! This stark difference is partly due to the stronger community ties you find in smaller towns. These close-knit communities foster social cohesion and mutual support, naturally deterring crime and creating a stronger sense of security. It's a safety net that's often missing in the anonymity of large cities.

So, what does this all mean? Escaping big cities offers young people a path towards a more affordable, healthier, and safer future. It allows them to build a strong financial foundation through accessible homeownership, enjoy the physical and mental health benefits of readily available nature, and live in communities where safety and security are not a constant worry. We urge you to support this motion and recognize the transformative potential of escaping big cities for the future of our young people.
