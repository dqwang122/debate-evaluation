Welcome to the Debate Evaluation!

You'll be evaluating a 20-minute debate where two sides discuss a topic. Your opinion matters - you'll vote both before and after listening the debate. We will use your feedback to improve the debate quality.

What to Expect:

  • Listen to a debate divided into Opening (4 min per side), Rebuttal (4 min per side), and Closing stages (2 min per side).
  • Share your initial opinion before listening.
  • Evaluate how each side performs during the debate.
  • Cast your final vote after listening.
  • The process will take about 1 hour and a $30 Amazon gift card will be sent via email as a gift for your participation.

How to Vote:

  • Support "For" Side - If you agree with the motion
  • Support "Against" Side - If you disagree with the motion
  • Choose "Tie" - If you do not have a preference

Rating Guide:

Poor (1)

Claims lack evidence/reasoning or contradictory evidence. Tone is flat/disengaging with no audience awareness.

Weak (2)

Limited evidence with poor organization and inconsistent emotional delivery.

Moderate (3)

On-topic evidence with logical flow and balanced emotional tone showing basic audience awareness, moderately swaying your opinion toward the given position.

Strong (4)

Substantial evidence and clear logic. Engaging language that resonates with audience value. Substantially shift your perspective, though minor gaps may remain.

Compelling (5)

Powerful evidence with effective counterpoints and masterful emotional appeal that creates deep connection. Strongly convince you to support the given position.

In this debate, we shall argue that escaping from big cities, defined as relocating from major metropolitan areas to smaller towns or rural communities, is the way out, meaning a path towards greater overall well-being, for today's young people (individuals aged 18-35).

First, escaping big cities offers a more affordable and less stressful path to financial stability for young people. For example, a comparative study by Saving to Invest showed that a monthly budget of $4,000 in a small town provides a comparable lifestyle to a $10,000 budget in a big city (2). This financial stability reduces stress and enables entrepreneurial pursuits, directly contributing to overall well-being. Furthermore, programs designed to attract young professionals, like remote work incentives and business grants, are increasingly common in smaller communities, furthering their financial prospects.

Second, escaping big cities promotes better mental and physical health. Studies show urban dwellers have a 20% higher risk of developing depression compared to those in rural areas (3). Smaller communities offer stronger social connections, mitigating the loneliness often experienced in large cities. Additionally, increased access to nature, with its proven benefits for stress reduction and physical activity (4), further enhances both mental and physical well-being. This is crucial to the overall well-being of young people, allowing them to thrive in a healthier environment. For instance, access to hiking trails, parks, and open spaces is typically far greater in less densely populated areas, providing opportunities for a more active and nature-connected lifestyle.

Third, escaping big cities fosters greater personal growth and fulfillment. While big cities may seem to offer more career options, the intense competition can be detrimental to personal development. Smaller towns often foster deeper community involvement, providing opportunities for leadership roles and contributing to local initiatives. This sense of belonging and purpose, demonstrated by higher volunteerism rates in smaller communities (Evidence Needed), contributes significantly to overall well-being. The focus shifts from individual career advancement to a more holistic approach to personal growth, embracing diverse opportunities for contribution and connection. This, in turn, leads to a richer, more meaningful life experience.

In conclusion, escaping big cities provides a pathway to enhanced overall well-being for young people through improved financial stability, better mental and physical health, and greater personal growth and fulfillment.

This directly addresses *why* overall well-being is a more important metric, emphasizing that a fulfilling life encompasses far more than just career success. Escaping big cities allows young people to prioritize these crucial elements, creating a truly successful and meaningful life.
