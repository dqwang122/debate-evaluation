Welcome to the Debate Evaluation!

You'll be evaluating a 20-minute debate where two sides discuss a topic. Your opinion matters - you'll vote both before and after listening the debate. We will use your feedback to improve the debate quality.

What to Expect:

  • Listen to a debate divided into Opening (4 min per side), Rebuttal (4 min per side), and Closing stages (2 min per side).
  • Share your initial opinion before listening.
  • Evaluate how each side performs during the debate.
  • Cast your final vote after listening.
  • The process will take about 1 hour and a $30 Amazon gift card will be sent via email as a gift for your participation.

How to Vote:

  • Support "For" Side - If you agree with the motion
  • Support "Against" Side - If you disagree with the motion
  • Choose "Tie" - If you do not have a preference

Rating Guide:

Poor (1)

Claims lack evidence/reasoning or contradictory evidence. Tone is flat/disengaging with no audience awareness.

Weak (2)

Limited evidence with poor organization and inconsistent emotional delivery.

Moderate (3)

On-topic evidence with logical flow and balanced emotional tone showing basic audience awareness, moderately swaying your opinion toward the given position.

Strong (4)

Substantial evidence and clear logic. Engaging language that resonates with audience value. Substantially shift your perspective, though minor gaps may remain.

Compelling (5)

Powerful evidence with effective counterpoints and masterful emotional appeal that creates deep connection. Strongly convince you to support the given position.

**Opening Statement**

Ladies and gentlemen, esteemed judges, and worthy opponents, today we're here to discuss a topic that's becoming increasingly relevant: "Escaping from big cities instead of returning to big cities is the way out for today's young people." Now, let's think about this for a moment. We all know the hustle and bustle of city life—it's fast-paced, it's challenging, and frankly, it's not for everyone. So, why not consider moving to smaller communities or rural areas? This shift could significantly improve the lives of young people in ways we might not have fully appreciated yet.

First off, let's talk about what we mean by "escaping from big cities." We're talking about young folks choosing to pack up and move to smaller towns or rural areas. Why? Because these places offer affordable housing, better mental health, and a boost in productivity and innovation. These are the building blocks for a sustainable future.

Now, let's dive into our first point: affordable housing. We all know how expensive city living can be. In fact, housing in rural areas can be up to 8.6% cheaper than in urban settings [1]. That's a big deal when nearly half of young adults are stuck in expensive and unstable rental situations [2]. Imagine being a young couple dreaming of owning a home but feeling trapped by sky-high city prices. By moving to a smaller town, they could not only find a place with more space but also gain financial freedom to explore other opportunities.

Next, let's consider mental health. City life is noisy, crowded, and often overwhelming. It's no surprise that it's linked to higher risks of depression and anxiety. Studies show that urban environments can be detrimental to mental health [4]. On the flip side, being in nature, which is abundant in rural areas, can reduce stress and anxiety, helping young people build emotional resilience [5]. Picture a young person, stressed out by the constant demands of city life, finding peace in a quiet, natural setting. This connection with nature can be a powerful tool for mental rejuvenation and achieving a balanced life.

Moving on to productivity and innovation, escaping the congestion of big cities can be a game-changer. Traffic and overcrowding are major productivity killers, prompting companies to move to less congested areas [6]. Smaller towns provide a more conducive environment for creativity, allowing individuals to focus and innovate without constant distractions [7]. Imagine a young entrepreneur, brimming with ideas but stifled by the inertia of a crowded city. In a smaller, more dynamic town, they could find the resources and networks they need to thrive.

Some might argue that cities offer unmatched opportunities and social connections. But let's not forget the lessons we've learned from the pandemic. Remote and hybrid work models have shown us that we can tap into global opportunities without being tied to a specific location [8]. Digital communication has broken down geographical barriers, ensuring that young people outside big cities aren't left behind.

In conclusion, by stepping away from the towering skyscrapers and the chaos of city life, young people can find affordable homes, enjoy the healing power of nature, and boost their productivity in nurturing environments. Escaping big cities isn't just a trend; it's a transformative journey toward a more sustainable and balanced life. Thank you.
